I work on advanced manufacturing, system integration, and data-driven experimentation, with a focus on robotic forming, texture-induced morphing, and process reliability.

Selected Projects

  • Texture-Induced Morphing – Engineered surface textures using Incremental Sheet Forming to embed residual stress patterns and achieve controlled morphing of sheet metal structures.
  • Robotic Incremental Sheet Forming Platforms – Developed robotic forming systems integrating UR5 arms, force sensing, and adaptive toolpath control for high-precision, flexible forming.
  • Sapphire Scratching & X-ray Nanoprobe Analysis – Designed experiments and analysis workflows to connect mechanical loading with subsurface strain fields.
  • G-code Generation & Visualization Tools – Built Python tools for custom toolpath generation and motion visualization for forming and texturing processes.

Research & Interests

My work spans incremental forming, residual stress engineering, reliability of formed structures, and the integration of experiments with simulation and data analysis. I am especially interested in how process design, texture, and loading conditions can be used to control shape, performance, and durability of components.
