Texture-Induced Morphing for Programmable Sheet Metal Structures

Ph.D. Research Assistant · Northwestern University · Aug 2024 – Present

Developed a method to use Incremental Sheet Forming (ISF) to embed residual stress patterns via engineered surface textures, enabling sheet metal to morph into target geometries when boundary conditions are modified. The work combines finite-strain plate theory with optimization algorithms to design texture layouts that minimize shape error under given constraints.

  • Applied ISF to create tailored surface textures that drive controlled morphing after forming.
  • Built an analytical–experimental workflow to correlate morphing behavior with texture geometry and forming parameters.
  • Used inverse design and optimization to infer texture patterns from desired final shapes.

Mold Liners Produced by Incremental Sheet Forming

Ph.D. Research Assistant · Northwestern University · Jun 2024 – Mar 2025 · Published in CIRP Annals

Investigated Incremental Sheet Forming as a method for producing mold liners for small-scale molding systems. Designed toolpaths based on tooltip geometry and feature skeletons to create high-quality textures and features on thin metal liners used with PDMS and other casting materials.

  • Developed toolpath strategies to control texture fidelity and geometric accuracy on formed liners.
  • Demonstrated improved demolding performance and surface finish compared to SLA-printed molds.
  • Showed potential for broader material compatibility and low-volume, rapidly reconfigurable tooling.

Representative publication: Mold liners produced by incremental sheet forming

Geometric Accuracy in Incremental Sheet Forming – ESAFORM Benchmark 2024

Ph.D. Research Assistant · Northwestern University · Oct 2023 – Apr 2025 · Multi-institution study with 13 partners

Contributed to a large-scale benchmark study on the geometric accuracy of complex shapes formed by Single-Point Incremental Forming (SPIF), Two-Point Incremental Forming (TPIF), and Double-Sided Incremental Forming (DSIF). Led DSIF experiments, coordinated data collection, and analyzed deviations across processes.

  • Executed controlled forming experiments using multiple ISF configurations on a shared benchmark geometry.
  • Processed and compared geometric data from 13 institutions to identify trends in accuracy and process capability.
  • Helped establish shared protocols and data standards for future ISF benchmarking efforts.

Representative publication: ESAFORM benchmark 2024: study on the geometric accuracy of a complex shape with single point incremental forming

Hierarchical Surface Textures for Improved Coating Durability (SLIPS)

Ph.D. Research Assistant · Northwestern University · Aug 2023 – Dec 2024 · Published in Journal of Manufacturing Science and Engineering

Studied the role of surface texturing via DSIF in enhancing the durability of Slippery Liquid-Infused Porous Surfaces (SLIPS) on metal substrates. Developed a process that integrates shape forming and surface texturing using universal tools to simplify manufacturing.

  • Investigated discretized dimple textures and their effect on lubricant retention and self-healing behavior.
  • Showed that low-speed tool spinning improves surface smoothness and hydrophobicity under sliding contact.
  • Demonstrated longer coating life and better performance under demanding environmental conditions.

Representative publication: Hierarchical surface textures for improved coating durability using DSIF

Robotic English Wheel Forming Automation

Ph.D. Research Assistant · Northwestern University · Aug 2023 – Jun 2024

Designed and implemented a robotic English wheel system that integrates UR5 robots, a custom gripper, real-time force sensing, and adaptive toolpath control to automate a traditionally manual forming process.

  • Developed the mechanical design, sensing architecture, and control logic for robotic English wheel forming.
  • Improved repeatability and process understanding relative to purely manual operation.
  • Mentored undergraduate students in design, prototyping, and system validation.

DSIF Manufacturing of Functional Shell Structures for UUV Hulls

Ph.D. Research Assistant · Northwestern University · Sept 2021 – Jun 2023

Developed Double-Sided Incremental Forming (DSIF) methods to fabricate shell structures with integrated drag-reducing and anti-biofouling textures for unmanned underwater vehicle (UUV) hulls. Demonstrated DSIF as an agile, tool-free process for low-volume production.

  • Implemented dual toolpath strategies to form riblet textures on curved shell geometries.
  • Integrated SLIPS coatings to enhance hydrodynamic performance and fouling resistance.
  • Showcased digitally driven, on-demand manufacturing for functional marine components.

Representative publication: Toolmarks-driven surface texture for coating attachment with drag reduction and anti-biofouling performance

Robotic Power Hammer Forming & Diagnostics at General Motors

Visiting Researcher · General Motors · Aug 2022 – Jun 2023

Automated a traditional power hammer forming process by integrating FANUC robotics with finite element simulations, reducing dependence on scarce manual expertise and improving consistency. Designed a new experimental setup combining high-speed imaging and computer vision to measure impact speed and validate forming dynamics.

  • Developed robotic forming workflows that improved repeatability and reduced labor time.
  • Used Abaqus simulations to perform springback analysis and optimize process parameters.
  • Communicated findings to engineers and managers, positioning robotic forming as a scalable digital manufacturing solution.

Frequency-Multiplexed Acoustic Tweezers for Microparticle Manipulation

Research Assistant · Duke University · Sept 2018 – Mar 2020

Designed and optimized 3D acoustic tweezers with innovative transducer layouts for dynamic manipulation of microparticles and cells. Combined simulation and experimental testing to implement frequency-multiplexed control, enabling tunable forces and higher throughput for lab-on-a-chip applications.

  • Developed device geometries and operating strategies for precise, contactless particle manipulation.
  • Integrated simulation, microfabrication, and testing in a full design loop.
  • Contributed to multiple journal papers in acoustofluidics and lab-on-a-chip technologies.

Representative publication: Acoustic tweezers based on circular, slanted-finger interdigital transducers for dynamic manipulation of micro-objects
